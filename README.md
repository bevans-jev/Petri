# Petri
How to play

Open petri.html in any web browser (just double-click it), or play the hosted link if someone shared one.


Your first piece must sit on your starting corner (the little colored wedge).
Every piece after that must touch one of your own pieces at a corner (the way two checkers squares meet diagonally) and must never sit flat beside your own color along a full edge. Touching other players, edge or corner, is fine.
If you can't fit anything, you're out and the others keep playing. When everyone's stuck, most squares wins.


On a phone: tap a piece, tap the board to drop it (red means illegal), nudge it with the arrows until it's legal, then Submit.

Scoring

1 point per square of your color on the board (including free squares from tiles or powers). Place all 21 of your pieces for a +15 bonus, and +5 more if your very last piece was the single square.

Setup options

Flip extra layers on or off at setup. Powers give each player a special ability; Faction traits give each country a built-in quirk; Events unleash board-wide disasters. Use the Pure shortcut for the classic game or Everything to stack it all on.

Special tiles (on some boards)


Teleport ,  When you cover it → 1 free square of your color appears on a random empty spot somewhere else.
Wall ,  When you cover it → up to 3 empty squares beside it become walls (blocked for everyone).
Bomb ,  When you cover it → every enemy square in the 8 cells around it is removed.


Powers (pick one)

You get one power with a few uses, spent from the panel on your turn. Powers only work in the early game, then they lock, so use them while they count. Hit Undo before placing a piece to take one back.


Seed ,  Adds 1 square of your color there.
Block ,  Turns that square into a wall.
Clear ,  Removes that square to open a corner.
Bridge ,  That piece may touch your color edge to edge.
Graft ,  Steals one rival square that touches your color, turning it into yours.
Spore ,  Instantly sprouts 2 new squares on open corners of your colony.
Blight ,  Removes one rival square anywhere on the board.


Countries & traits

Each player is a country with its own color. With traits on, every country also has a quirk (duplicates are fine, each works on its own):


Kyrgyzstan ,  Spore Burst: its Teleport tiles spawn 2 squares, not 1
Botswana ,  Bomb Shield: Bomb tiles never remove its squares
Lithuania ,  Outpost: plants 1 extra square out toward mid-board at the start
Guatemala ,  Late Bloomer: can use its power far later into the game than anyone else
Colombia ,  Stockpile: gets one extra power charge
Tonga ,  Big Bonus: +1 point for every 5-square piece it places
Malta ,  Bastion: Wall tiles can never turn its squares into walls
Dominican Republic ,  Cash Crop: scores 1 bonus point for every 4 pieces it places
Senegal ,  Long Coast: scores 1 bonus point for every long, stretched piece it places (4 or more cells in a straight line)


Events

Turn Events on and choose which are live. Every few rounds one strikes the whole board, some hit once, some linger. They don't play favorites.


Meteor Strike ,  Rocks slam the dish, craters vaporize squares (yours, theirs, even walls) and leave permanent rubble behind.
Earthquake ,  Fissures tear across the board; anything on a fault line falls in. Permanent.
Bloom ,  A growth surge, every surviving colony sprouts a couple of free squares.
Infection ,  A blight starts in an empty patch and creeps outward each round, slowly eating any squares it touches. Build away from it.


CPU opponents


Easy ,  Plays loose, close to random.
Medium ,  Grabs the biggest shapes it can fit.
Defensive ,  Fortifies its own territory and holds the line. Solid, but plays it safe.
Aggressive ,  Drives deep into rival ground and chokes their corners shut.
Petri ,  The complete player. Spreads efficiently, denies rivals, and uses its powers well. Wins the most.


Boards

Open Range is the standard 20×20. Obstacle boards keep most of the space open. Mega maps are city-scale: everyone gets two of every piece, best with 3 to 4 players.


Open Range ,  20×20 · open
The Cage ,  20×20 · walls

The Dish ,  26×26 · round dish
